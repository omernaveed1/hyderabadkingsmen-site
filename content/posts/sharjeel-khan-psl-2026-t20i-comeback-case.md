Sharjeel Khan has never done things quietly. When he bats well, the noise is immediate, visceral, and — for Pakistan's T20I selectors — increasingly difficult to ignore. His form in PSL 2026 has reopened a conversation that never really closed.

## The Resurgence Is Real

Sharjeel is not merely accumulating runs in PSL 2026. He is doing what he has always done best: attacking the powerplay with a hostility that very few openers anywhere in the world can match. The left-hander's strike rate has been at the level that made him a white-ball phenomenon before his five-year ban derailed everything. Boundaries off both pace and spin, a willingness to go after the first ball of the innings, and the kind of physical presence at the crease that unsettles bowlers before a delivery is even bowled.

The numbers are backing the eye test. He is among the top run-scorers in the tournament through the early rounds, and more importantly, the tempo at which those runs are coming is PSL-best standard, not just respectable for his age bracket.

## What This Story Is Actually About

This is not simply a comeback narrative. Pakistan cricket has watched this film before. Sharjeel returns, dominates domestic white-ball cricket, generates headlines, and then the selectorial door stays firmly shut.

The real question is whether PSL 2026 represents a tipping point or another false dawn.

At 35, Sharjeel is not rebuilding a career. He is making a final, legitimate argument. And the argument has genuine weight. Pakistan's T20I top order has carried structural uncertainty for the better part of two years. Mohammad Rizwan leads from the front, Babar Azam's T20I relationship with aggressive intent at the top remains a subject of fierce debate, and the left-hand right-hand combination question at the top of the order keeps circling back. Sharjeel, a destructive left-hander, answers at least one of those problems.

The other dimension here is what this says about Pakistan's domestic structure. If a player can be this effective in a high-quality franchise tournament and still not earn a national call-up, it raises fair questions about what selectors are actually prioritising. Are they looking for peak PSL form? International temperament? Age? Youth development? The criteria need to be transparent, because right now they appear inconsistent.

## What It Means Going Forward

For Sharjeel personally, sustaining this form over the full PSL 2026 campaign is non-negotiable. A hot start that fizzles will simply confirm the doubts around his consistency at this stage of his career. He needs to finish the tournament the way he is starting it.

For the selectors, the calculus is uncomfortable. Pakistan face a busy T20I calendar and the next ICC T20 World Cup cycle is already building. If the philosophy is aggressive, high-intent batting from ball one in the powerplay, then Sharjeel Khan in 2026 form is exactly that template. Ignoring him while he is the most destructive powerplay batter in the PSL would require an explanation.

There is also a franchise dimension. Whichever PSL side has Sharjeel in their XI this season is benefiting from a player performing well above what many expected at this stage. It puts pressure on rival franchises' opening combinations and gives his team a consistent powerplay platform that most sides in the league would take without hesitation.

## What to Watch Next

Watch how Sharjeel performs against the tournament's best pace attacks, specifically on slower Karachi and Multan surfaces where the ball does not come on as freely. The easy runs against ordinary bowling will not settle the debate. His performances in knockout-pressure scenarios and against Pakistan's genuine international-quality fast bowlers in the PSL will carry the most weight.

Also watch the national selectors' public communication. If they continue to say nothing about Sharjeel while he dominates the tournament, that silence will itself be a statement, and not a flattering one. Pakistan cricket has a history of brilliant misfits who deserved more chances than they received. Sharjeel Khan spent years as the defining example of that category. In 2026, with a bat in his hand and runs on the board, he is making the case that the category still applies.
