The Hyderabad Kingsmen lost their first PSL match by 41 runs to Lahore Qalandars at Gaddafi Stadium. They lost the second to Multan Sultans. They lost the third to Quetta Gladiators and the fourth to Rawalpindiz, the other PSL 2026 expansion team. Four games, four losses, every one of them by a margin that wasn't close.

If you read the Pakistan cricket press in the third week of March 2026, the consensus was settled: this was a debut team that had bought a big name in Marnus Labuschagne, a respected coach in Jason Gillespie, and not much else. The squad looked thin. The captain was new to T20 captaincy. Six weeks until the season ended in irrelevance.

Six weeks later, Hyderabad Kingsmen were in the PSL final.

## How the corner turned

The first win came against Karachi Kings, at Niaz Stadium. Saim Ayub made 78 off 41 at the top, Sharjeel Khan finished it with 30 not out, and Riley Meredith took 3 for 22 with the new ball. The result mattered less than the way it was won. The Kingsmen had spent four games trying to win every category — out-batting the opposition, out-bowling them, out-fielding them, all at once. Against Karachi, the team played a simple game: keep wickets in hand for Ayub at the top, give Meredith and Akif Javed clear roles with the new ball, let Labuschagne anchor at three.

The pattern stuck. Hyderabad won six of the next seven group games. Saim Ayub finished the league stage as the second-highest run-scorer in the PSL, behind only Rizwan Mehmood, who plays for the Kingsmen too. Riley Meredith took the second-most wickets. Labuschagne batted at three almost every match and made 360 runs at a strike rate of 134 — not flashy, but the average that held the order together.

Fourth place in the league stage. Ten points. Into the playoffs as the lowest seed.

## The playoffs

Eliminator 1, against Multan Sultans at the Gaddafi. The Kingsmen had lost to Multan in their second league game, by 65 runs in a result that felt like a relegation. The rematch was an eight-wicket win. Sharjeel Khan made 64 off 38 in the chase. Hammad Azam, who had played one league game all season, bowled four overs for 18 in the squeeze.

Eliminator 2, against Islamabad United, the league-stage table-toppers. United posted 174 in their 20. Hyderabad lost early wickets in the chase and went into the 20th over needing 11 to win with two wickets in hand. Akif Javed, batting at number 9, hit a 6 off the third ball and squeezed two off the fourth. Two runs to win, one wicket left. Tayyab Arif, last man in, hit the ball back at the bowler and ran. The throw missed the stumps. Two runs, one wicket — Hyderabad won by two runs.

The Pakistan cricket media has called the Eliminator 2 win the "Niaz Miracle," even though the match wasn't played at Niaz. The Pakistan Cricket Board officially shared the broadcast highlight at three minutes past midnight that night. By morning it had a million views.

## The final

Peshawar Zalmi at the National Stadium, Karachi, on 3 May 2026. Peshawar had been the form team of the back half of the league stage and won the playoffs comfortably. Hyderabad had been the story team.

The Kingsmen batted first and made 162. It was 30-40 under what the surface allowed. Saim Ayub fell early to Mohammed Amir for a duck. Labuschagne made 38 but slowed in the middle, scoring at a strike rate of 110 across his innings. Sharjeel Khan provided the only real acceleration with 42 off 22 at the death.

Peshawar chased it with five wickets to spare. Babar Azam made 67 not out. The game was over by the 18th over. Hyderabad finished as runners-up in their debut PSL season.

## What it means going forward

The story of a debut team finishing as runners-up is, on its own, one of the great PSL stories. The story of a debut team going 0-4 and then making the final from there is unprecedented in any of the world's domestic T20 leagues. What the Kingsmen built in IPL 2026 is a season that should be studied for years.

The hard question for 2027 is the squad. Labuschagne's contract is for one year only, with an option for a second. Riley Meredith and Ottneil Baartman are similarly short-term overseas signings. Saim Ayub's value has gone through the roof and he'll attract bids from every league globally. Whether the Kingsmen can keep enough of the 2026 group together to defend the runner-up finish is the season's biggest off-field question.

The other question is whether the team has found an identity. The 0-4 start to 2026 felt like a side trying to play four different brands of cricket. The unbeaten finish to the league stage felt like a side that had finally settled on one. Whatever that one is — the patient Labuschagne anchor, the Ayub-Sharjeel powerplay, the Meredith new-ball discipline — that's what needs to walk into PSL 2027.

The Kingsmen earned a foothold this season. Whether they keep it depends on what happens between now and February.
