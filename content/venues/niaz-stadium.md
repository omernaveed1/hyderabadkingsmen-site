Niaz Stadium sits in the old part of Hyderabad, Sindh — a city that has had cricket grounds for over a century but, until 2026, no franchise of its own to call them home. The Hyderabad Kingsmen's arrival as a PSL franchise in their debut 2026 season made Niaz a top-flight cricket venue again after years of hosting only domestic matches.

## A short history

Niaz Stadium has been Hyderabad's main cricket ground since the 1950s. It hosted four Tests for Pakistan between 1973 and 1984 — the last of them a draw against India in March 1983 in which Wasim Akram, then yet to debut, watched from the boundary as a 16-year-old at Sindh trials.

For most of the 21st century, Niaz hosted Quaid-e-Azam Trophy fixtures for Sindh-based teams and Pakistan Cup matches, but no top-tier international or franchise cricket. The Pakistan Cricket Board's 2024-25 venue infrastructure upgrade — driven in part by the PSL expansion talks that would eventually create the Kingsmen and the Rawalpindiz — funded a substantial renovation. New floodlights, refurbished player areas, improved drainage, and an expanded media facility were completed in time for the 2026 PSL season.

## The pitch

Niaz plays slower than most PSL pitches. The surface has historically gripped for spin from the middle overs onwards and most matches here have finished with totals in the 140-170 range rather than the 180+ that Karachi and Lahore tend to produce.

What makes it work for the Kingsmen is the composition of their squad. Hassan Khan and Maaz Sadaqat — both left-arm spinners — get more turn here than they do at any other PSL venue. Akif Javed's variations, particularly his slower-ball cutter, hold their grip on the surface in a way they don't on a true Karachi pitch. The Kingsmen's bowling identity, built on cutters and grip rather than pace and bounce, fits Niaz almost perfectly.

The toss matters here more than at most PSL venues. The dew sets in by the 35th over of evening matches, and the team batting second has a measurable advantage when the ball gets wet. Toss-winning captains chose to bowl first in 5 of the 7 Kingsmen home games in PSL 2026.

## The launch ceremony

On 7 February 2026, Niaz hosted the Kingsmen's team-name and logo reveal. The ceremony was attended by Sindh's chief minister, the FKS consortium ownership, Jason Gillespie, and several Pakistan international cricketers. The black-and-gold colour scheme was revealed alongside the team's warrior-themed logo — a golden crown and gladiator helmet that has, in the months since, become one of the most recognisable franchise marks in Pakistan cricket.

The choice of Niaz for the launch rather than the more conventional venues (a hotel ballroom in Karachi or a PCB press room in Lahore) was deliberate. The Kingsmen ownership wanted the franchise to feel grounded in Hyderabad rather than parachuted in. By holding the launch at the stadium where the team would play, with a Hyderabad crowd, they made a statement about identity that has paid off across the season in fan engagement and home-game attendance.

## Notable PSL 2026 matches

**Kingsmen 167/4 vs Karachi Kings 156 all out, 18 March 2026.** Hyderabad's first PSL win, on home turf. Saim Ayub 78 off 41, Riley Meredith 3/22. The crowd at Niaz that night, in a stadium that had been waiting two seasons of build-up to see top-flight cricket, was the loudest in any of the team's 2026 fixtures.

**Kingsmen 162 all out vs Islamabad United 161/9, 6 April 2026.** The one-run win against the league-toppers. Hyderabad scrambled to 162 batting first, then defended it through a death-overs masterclass from Akif Javed (4-0-28-3, including the final-over wicket).

**Kingsmen 145 all out vs Quetta Gladiators 147/3, 22 March 2026.** The one Kingsmen home loss of the league stage. Mohammed Hasnain bowled 4-0-19-3 for Quetta on a slow Niaz pitch that suited his cutters perfectly — proof that the venue helps any team with a cutter-heavy attack, not just the home side.

## If you're going to a match

The stadium is in the old part of Hyderabad, off Tilak Incline. From Karachi, it's a 2.5-hour drive on the M9 motorway and Niaz has its own car-park for around 800 vehicles. From central Hyderabad, the stadium is a 15-minute auto-rickshaw ride from the Latifabad area.

Arrive at least 90 minutes early — security checks at Niaz are now PSL-standard, which means thorough. The North Stand has the best atmosphere; the East Stand has the best afternoon shade. Concessions inside the ground are limited; the row of dhabas outside Gate 3 serves the best chai and biryani in walking distance.
